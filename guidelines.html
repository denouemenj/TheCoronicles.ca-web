GUIDELINES

We value originality, creativity, and effort.

GENERAL GUIDLINES

Our core values of this project includes...

  1. ORIGINALITY — We only accept original art created by you that reflects your own experience as a person. Make it personal, make it original. Anything found online or a modified version of another artwork may not be submitted.
  2. RESPECT — We respect your art piece and the part of your life that you are so generous to share with us. However, we also ask you to respect the people mentioned in your art and their right to not be in public attention.
  3. ON-TOPIC — Our intention for this project is to make it so people can share their experience during this global pandemic, so make sure your art is related and on-topic.
  4. DEMOGRAPHIC— We have launched our project for the youth of Canada. You will have to be between the age of 12 to 24 at before February 01 midnight in order to submit.

VISUAL ART GUIDELINES

  1. FORMAT - We will accept visual art forms such as painting, sketch, photography, graphics, and anything you can send to us digitally.
  2. QUALITY - Your uploaded art needs to be at least 700 * 700 px resolution to make sure that we can print your piece clearly on paper. For digital art, vector files are preferred.
  3. INFORMATION - Make sure to include a title and short description for your art piece, elaborate on why this piece of art means so much to you during COVID, 150 words minimum.

LITERATURE GUIDELINES

  1. FORMAT - We accept literature forms such as poetry, journal, essay, or any format you are comfortable with. Make sure to include a tittle.
  2. FILE TYPE - Make sure you upload in a .pdf file.
  3. LENGTH - Your submitted literature should have a maximum of 700 words.

Writing Prompts:

What has been the biggest change in your life?
Has anything positive happened because of the pandemic?
What does you life in lock down look like?
Online school and work from home experiences.
Share your story of being laid off and how that has changed your life.
